### Reframes that reordered everything

These conceptual moves changed how the work was understood. Operating without them risks falling back into pre-reframe patterns.

1. **Stop bundle-by-bundle, do one structural stage per session** (Arc 1, May 11). Bundle approach fragmented the engine work; structural-per-session let each stage's discipline land cleanly. Many bundle-level findings dissolved as side effects.
2. **Compression as design, not bug** (Arc 2, May 18). Caught Claude mid-design about to ship WITHIN-CAP DISCRIMINATION that would have pushed MD scores DOWN from 6.0 toward 5.0-5.5. Most ideas SHOULD land at the cap; compression is calibrated discipline. Stopped before applying the wrong fix.
3. **Book-cover frame for scores** (Arc 2, May 18). Scores are book covers; prose is the first pages. Prose is the trust mechanism, not the score number. Established Type 1 user (score-driven) and Type 2 user (prose-driven) as co-equal — Stream A and Stream B as parallel work, not sequenced.
4. **Token-order revelation** (Arc 2, May 19). "How do you score an idea? You first list and think about your evidence and input." Score + prose are co-decided at token-emission time when JSON schema emits score before explanation. Model forms gestalt, emits score, then rationalizes. Unlocked V4S31 two-call physical separation as structural answer.
5. **Within-profile as primary UX verification target** (Arc 2, May 18). Cross-profile = QA infrastructure; within-profile = product signal. Reordered every priority.
6. **Justifiability over consistency** (Arc 3, May 20). Tested all 6 V4S31 spikes against the bar — none passed. The spike rate WAS a real problem under Emre's own bar even though it was 17% (low under consistency framing). Reset the success bar.
7. **Mechanism change > careful generation on diminishing-returns curve** (Arc 3, May 21). Three architectural moves hit same 17% residual. Answer is a different mechanism class (Prosecutor + Repair), not another constraint layer.
8. **Mechanical lookup beats careful generation at the judgment layer** (Arc 4, May 29). Generalization of #7 one level deeper. When *careful generation itself* is the residual — when adversarial audit has working catch-rate, signal the underlying decision is still wobbling — the next mechanism class is to *remove the judgment*. Replace model score-choice with a deterministic function of predicate commitments. The model still judges predicates (where it's good), the system computes score (where the model rationalizes). The disease class moves from "model picks score then rationalizes prose" to structurally impossible.
9. **The wobble was upstream, and determinism was the wrong goal** (Arc 5, June 2). A week aimed at "fix Stage 2a" resolved, via the freeze ladder, to: Stage 1 (an LLM call) is the noise source and 2a merely amplifies it; you cannot make any LLM stage deterministic (temp 0 ≠ deterministic, proven for Haiku keywords AND Sonnet Stage 1). The reframe that ended the chase: stop optimizing for identical reruns; optimize for **justified, traceable variance.** Same idea → slightly different but each-individually-defensible score, with prose that explains it, is the product working — not failing. The only real defects are untraceable outputs. This converted "the engine is unstable, rebuild it" into "the engine is fine, fix two emission bugs and ship."

10. **You can't have fine + traceable + LLM-only — the design triangle** (Arc 6, June 4). The fine-scoring chase ended here: the model genuinely has the resolution (it commits distinct sums for distinct evidence) but cannot reliably *execute* the arithmetic that expresses it (MO fine table 46% off-table live). The reframe: it was never the architecture or the design choice that blocked finer scores — it was putting the deterministic arithmetic inside a stochastic executor. IdeaLoop already does code-side scoring (the overall score in `scoring.js`), so the missing fine-grained corner is reachable by moving per-metric derivation there too. The high-trust design (traceable, evidence-bound) is right; its cost is only that you can't let the model invent precision — and the cure is to compute precision in code, not to abandon traceability or chase determinism.

11. **Two cognitive modes, and the product is a second habitat — not a verdict machine** (V16 reset, June 10). The Free/Pro tier frame collapsed into a deeper one: the product holds TWO complementary modes — **Explore** (widen the possibility space, think WITH the founder, treat uncertainty as possibility) and **Deep** (narrow to a verdict, prosecute the idea, treat uncertainty as risk) — reverse-engineered from each other as negative space, not arranged as a hierarchy. The reframe behind the reframe: ILC is not a one-shot evaluator a founder visits once per idea; it is a **second cognitive habitat** they return to across an idea's whole life ("once an idea always an idea"). That reorders the launch list around building the workspace first and the habitat as destination, and it makes the scope boundary — **"we are the idea part"** — the load-bearing wall that keeps the habitat from sprawling into execution / marketing / distribution. The trap it guards against: letting Explore drift back into being Deep's mirror (a rubric, a verdict, a brief) instead of its complement — symmetry-thinking is the pull, negative-space is the discipline.

## Update protocol

This document is the operational source of truth for the action queue between sessions. **Update at the end of every session** — even partially completed ones.

### End-of-session update checklist

1. **Mark completed items** as ✅ DONE with date in Status column
2. **Mark in-progress items** with brief handoff context (what's done, what's left, blockers)
3. **Reorder queue** if priorities shifted during session; note why in change log below
4. **Add new items** discovered during session with rationale + suggested priority slot
5. **Move killed items** to Killed section with reason
6. **Move parked items** to Parked section with re-evaluation trigger
7. **Update document version** (V1 → V2 → V3...) and "Last updated" date at top
8. **Update "Last update reason"** with one-line summary of what changed
9. **Add change log entry** at bottom with date + diff summary

### Status legend

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ✅ DONE | Shipped, with date. Stays in queue for context until next major reshuffle. |
| → NEXT | The immediate next action. Only one item carries this status at a time. |
| QUEUED | Next up after current work, in priority order. |
| 🔄 IN PROGRESS | Active work this session, with brief handoff note. |
| CONDITIONAL | Trigger condition specified; not active until trigger fires. |
| BLOCKED | Waiting on dependency; what's blocking documented in Notes column. |
| POST-EP | Slotted after Execution Plan build-out; needs real users or downstream infrastructure. |

### When to reorder

Reorder is legitimate when:
- Production data invalidates a priority assumption (e.g., the score-shape guard starts firing in production — calibration of the offending scorer takes priority over queued feature work)
- A blocker resolves and unblocks higher-value work
- A new item's urgency exceeds current queue ordering
- A session reveals scope creep or dependency surprise

Reorder is NOT legitimate when:
- Energy/preference of the moment (work the queue, not the mood)
- A shiny new idea (file it as new item with rationale, don't preempt current next)
- Mid-session pivots without an explicit "pause + reorder" decision

### Document version anchoring

Each version increment is a session-boundary snapshot. The document is the source of truth between sessions, NOT during sessions. During a session, decisions can flow naturally; at session close, they get codified here.
